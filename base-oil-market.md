<p><p>Base oil markets are categorized into several groups based on the oil's refining process and properties. Group I includes solvent-refined oils with lower purity. Group II features hydroprocesses for improved stability and performance. Group III oils are hydrocracked and have higher viscosity indices, often considered synthetic. Group IV consists of polyalphaolefins (PAOs), fully synthetic with excellent temperature stability. Group V encompasses other synthetic and renewable oils that don't fit into the previous categories, offering unique properties for specialized applications.</p></p>
