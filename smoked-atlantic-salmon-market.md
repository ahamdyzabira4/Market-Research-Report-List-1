<p><p>The smoked Atlantic salmon market is primarily divided into hot smoking and cold smoking segments. Hot smoking involves curing and cooking the fish at higher temperatures, resulting in a flaky texture and robust flavor, making it ideal for ready-to-eat products. In contrast, cold smoking cures the fish at lower temperatures, preserving its silky texture and delicate taste, often used in gourmet applications. Both methods cater to distinct consumer preferences, influencing product offerings and market demand in the seafood sector.</p></p>
