<p><p>Fracking fluids are crucial in hydraulic fracturing, and they come in various types. Water-based fluids, the most commonly used, consist mainly of water mixed with additives to enhance efficiency. Oil-gel based fluids utilize oils and gelling agents for improved viscosity and stability, making them suitable for specific geological conditions. Foam-based fluids combine gas and liquid, reducing water usage and enabling effective gas delivery in low-permeability formations. Each type serves distinct purposes in optimizing extraction processes and enhancing well productivity.</p></p>
